Education

Oh, education. The magical journey where you spend years of your life memorizing facts you'll never use, mastering skills you'll never need, and taking tests that measure how well you can regurgitate information. Let's not forget the joy of student loans, because who doesn't love paying off debt well into their 40s? High school is particularly enriching. Nothing quite like learning trigonometry when all you really need to know is how to calculate a 15% tip. And who could forget the thrill of reading Shakespearean English, because understanding 16th-century drama is totally essential for navigating the 21st-century job market. And college? What a bargain! Spend thousands of dollars for the privilege of sitting in crowded lecture halls, listening to professors who may or may not be interested in teaching. It's not like you could learn the same things on YouTube for free or anything. And the cherry on top? A degree that guarantees nothing except the chance to apply for jobs that require "3-5 years of experience" for entry-level positions. So, here's to education! The perfect system for ensuring you spend your formative years preparing for a future that no one can predict and probably won't require half of what you learned. Cheers!
